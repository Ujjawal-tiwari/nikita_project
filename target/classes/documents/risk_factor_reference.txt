Internal Banking Reference Document

SECTION 1: KEY RISK FACTORS AND THEIR WEIGHTS

1.1 Payment History (Weight: 35%)
    - Most significant factor in credit scoring
    - On-time payments improve score, missed payments reduce significantly
    - Each missed payment can reduce score by 30-80 points
    - Recent missed payments have greater impact than older ones
    - Bankruptcy severely impacts score for 7-10 years

1.2 Credit Utilization / Debt-to-Income Ratio (Weight: 30%)
    - Measures total outstanding debt relative to income
    - Ideal DTI ratio: below 30%
    - High utilization (>50%) signals financial stress
    - Reducing outstanding balances improves score quickly

1.3 Credit History Length (Weight: 15%)
    - Longer credit history indicates stability
    - Minimum 3 years of credit history preferred for major loans
    - Average age of accounts matters
    - Closing old accounts can reduce credit history length and hurt score

1.4 Credit Mix (Weight: 10%)
    - Diverse credit portfolio (home loan + credit card + personal loan) viewed positively
    - Over-reliance on unsecured credit (credit cards, personal loans) is negative
    - Secured loans (home, auto) demonstrate asset-backed borrowing

1.5 New Credit Inquiries (Weight: 10%)
    - Multiple loan applications in short period reduce score
    - Each hard inquiry reduces score by 5-10 points
    - Rate shopping within 14-day window counted as single inquiry
    - Soft inquiries (self-checks) do not affect score

SECTION 2: RISK MITIGATION STRATEGIES FOR BORROWERS

2.1 Immediate Actions (1-3 months impact):
    - Set up automatic EMI payments to avoid missed payments
    - Pay more than minimum due on credit cards
    - Reduce credit card utilization below 30%

2.2 Medium-term Actions (3-12 months impact):
    - Consolidate high-interest debt into lower-interest options
    - Build emergency fund (3-6 months expenses)
    - Avoid applying for new credit unless necessary

2.3 Long-term Actions (12+ months impact):
    - Maintain consistent payment history
    - Diversify credit portfolio appropriately
    - Monitor credit report regularly for errors
    - Build savings and investment portfolio as collateral option

SECTION 3: RED FLAGS IN CREDIT ASSESSMENT

3.1 High-Risk Indicators:
    - DTI ratio above 50%
    - More than 2 missed payments in last 12 months
    - Credit score below 550
    - Multiple loan applications in 30 days
    - Active legal proceedings or SARFAESI notices
    - Listed as willful defaulter

3.2 Moderate-Risk Indicators:
    - DTI ratio between 40-50%
    - 1 missed payment in last 12 months
    - Credit score between 550-650
    - High credit card utilization
    - Recent employment change (within 6 months)
    - Self-employed without stable income documentation
