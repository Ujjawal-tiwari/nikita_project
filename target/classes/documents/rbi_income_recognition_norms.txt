=============================================================
RBI GUIDELINES ON INCOME RECOGNITION AND ASSET CLASSIFICATION
Reserve Bank of India - Prudential Norms (IRAC)
Circular No. RBI/2024-25/IR-02
=============================================================

SECTION 1: INCOME RECOGNITION NORMS

1.1 Income Recognition Policy:
    a) Income on Non-Performing Assets (NPAs) shall not be recognized on accrual basis.
    b) Interest on NPAs shall be recognized only on actual receipt (cash basis).
    c) Income from restructured accounts shall follow specific restructuring norms.

1.2 Interest Application on Loan Accounts:
    a) Interest shall be applied at quarterly intervals on performing accounts.
    b) For accounts classified as NPA, interest shall be reversed from profit.
    c) Penal interest charges must be transparent and communicated to borrowers.

SECTION 2: ASSET CLASSIFICATION NORMS

2.1 Standard Assets:
    - Accounts where principal and interest are current (not overdue beyond 90 days)
    - Provision requirement: 0.40% for retail, 0.75% for commercial

2.2 Sub-Standard Assets:
    - Assets which have remained NPA for a period not exceeding 12 months
    - Provision requirement: 15% of outstanding (25% for unsecured portion)

2.3 Doubtful Assets:
    - Assets which have remained in sub-standard category for 12 months
    - Period up to 1 year: 25% provision
    - Period 1-3 years: 40% provision
    - Period more than 3 years: 100% provision

2.4 Loss Assets:
    - Assets identified as uncollectable by bank or auditors
    - 100% provision required to be written off entirely

SECTION 3: NPA CLASSIFICATION CRITERIA

3.1 An asset becomes Non-Performing when:
    a) Interest or principal payment remains overdue for more than 90 days (retail/commercial)
    b) The account is classified as "out of order" for cash credit/overdraft facilities
    c) The bill remains overdue for more than 90 days in bill purchase/discount accounts
    d) Agricultural loan installment is overdue for 2 crop seasons (short) or 1 crop season (long)

3.2 Special Mention Accounts (SMA) Classification:
    - SMA-0: Principal/interest payment overdue up to 30 days
    - SMA-1: Principal/interest payment overdue 31-60 days
    - SMA-2: Principal/interest payment overdue 61-90 days

3.3 Impact of NPA Classification on Credit Score:
    - NPA classification significantly reduces CIBIL score (typically 50-100 point impact)
    - Resolution/upgrade of NPA takes minimum 6 months of regular payments
    - NPA history remains on credit report for 7 years
