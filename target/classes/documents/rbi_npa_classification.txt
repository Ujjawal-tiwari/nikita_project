=============================================================
RBI NPA CLASSIFICATION AND PROVISIONING NORMS
Reserve Bank of India - Master Direction on Prudential Norms
Circular No. RBI/2024-25/NPA-03
=============================================================

SECTION 1: DEFINITION AND IDENTIFICATION OF NPA

1.1 A loan or advance is classified as NPA when:
    - Interest and/or principal installment remains overdue for more than 90 days
    - The account remains continuously irregular as per sanctioned terms

1.2 Out of Order Status (for Cash Credit/Overdraft):
    - Outstanding balance exceeds sanctioned limit/drawing power continuously for 90 days
    - No credits in the account for 90 days, or credits are insufficient to cover interest

SECTION 2: PROVISIONING REQUIREMENTS

2.1 Standard Assets Provisioning:
    Category                          | Provision Rate
    ---------------------------------|---------------
    Direct Agriculture               | 0.25%
    SME Sector                       | 0.25%
    Commercial Real Estate (CRE)     | 1.00%
    CRE - Residential Housing        | 0.75%
    All Other Loans                   | 0.40%

2.2 Sub-Standard Assets: 15% provision (25% for unsecured portion)
2.3 Doubtful Assets - Secured Portion:
    - Up to 1 year: 25%
    - 1-3 years: 40%
    - More than 3 years: 100%
2.4 Doubtful Assets - Unsecured Portion: 100%
2.5 Loss Assets: 100% (to be written off or fully provided for)

SECTION 3: RESTRUCTURING OF STRESSED ASSETS

3.1 Banks may restructure loans of viable borrowers as per RBI's Resolution Framework.
3.2 Conditions for restructuring:
    a) Borrower must demonstrate viability and ability to service restructured debt
    b) Independent assessment of viability required for loans above Rs. 10 crore
    c) Restructured accounts to be classified as NPA unless upgraded through regular payments
    d) Provision on restructured standard assets: 5% (vs 0.40% for normal standard assets)

3.3 Willful Defaulters:
    - No additional facilities to be granted
    - Name to be reported to CIBIL and RBI
    - Penal provisions under relevant laws applicable
    - Credit score impact: Severe (score reduced to below 400)

SECTION 4: RECOVERY AND WRITE-OFF

4.1 Banks must initiate recovery proceedings within 90 days of NPA classification.
4.2 Recovery channels: SARFAESI Act, DRT, Lok Adalat, IBC proceedings.
4.3 Write-off does not extinguish the debt; recovery efforts must continue.
